Why Accessibility Matters

Approximately 15% of the world's population lives with some form of disability. That's over a billion people who might interact with your website differently. By designing with accessibility in mind, you're not only expanding your potential audience but also creating a better experience for all users.

Key Accessibility Principles

1. Color Contrast

Ensure sufficient contrast between text and background colors. The WCAG recommends at least 4.5:1 for normal text and 3:1 for large text. Tools like WebAIM's Contrast Checker can help verify your color choices.

2. Keyboard Navigation

All interactive elements should be accessible via keyboard alone. Test your site by navigating with the Tab key and ensure focus states are clearly visible.

3. Semantic HTML

Use proper HTML elements (like <button> for buttons and <nav> for navigation) to provide meaningful structure that screen readers can interpret.

4. Alternative Text

Always include descriptive alt text for images. For decorative images, use empty alt text (alt="") to indicate they can be ignored by screen readers.

Testing for Accessibility

Incorporate these tools into your workflow:

  • WAVE (Web Accessibility Evaluation Tool)
  • axe DevTools browser extension
  • VoiceOver (macOS) or NVDA (Windows) screen readers
  • Keyboard-only navigation testing

Remember, accessibility is an ongoing process, not a one-time checklist. By making it part of your design and development workflow, you'll create better experiences for everyone.
